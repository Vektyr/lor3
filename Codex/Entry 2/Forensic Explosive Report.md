# Forensic Explosive Report

FORENSIC EXPLOSIVE MATERIALS REPORT

Case Reference: [[Incident Overview Report|9912-47-L]]

Laboratory ID: CFX-8841
Requesting Unit: Outer Transit Patrol

Date Received: [REDACTED]

Date Completed: [REDACTED]

Submitted Materials:

— Fragmented metallic residues (vehicle structure)

— Soil samples from detonation center and periphery

— Trace particulate collected from debris field

— Swab samples from recovered remains and equipment fragments

Methodology:

— Gas Chromatography–Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS)

— Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR)

— Ion chromatography (IC)

— Scanning electron microscopy with energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (SEM-EDS)

— Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC)

Findings:

Explosive Classification

Analysis confirms the presence of a high-order explosive compound.

The explosive does not correspond to any commercially registered

civilian, mining, or industrial explosive formulation.

No direct match found in the Council Explosives Reference Database (CERD).

Chemical Composition (Detected Components)

Primary energetic compounds:

— Cyclotrimethylenetrinitramine (RDX): trace to moderate presence

— Pentaerythritol tetranitrate (PETN): trace presence

Secondary compounds:

— Stabilized nitrate esters

— Polymer-bonded energetic matrix (PBX-like structure)

Additives and modifiers:

— Phlegmatizing agents (exact formulation undetermined)

— Plasticizers consistent with controlled detonation shaping

— Micro-particulate aluminum oxide residues (oxidation byproduct)

Notably absent:

— Ammonium nitrate (AN)

— Fuel oil residues (FO)

— Chlorates or perchlorates

— Black powder constituents

Manufacturing Indicators

The detected compound exhibits:
— Homogeneous molecular distribution

— Absence of impurity clustering

— Controlled grain morphology

These characteristics are inconsistent with improvised explosive devices (IEDs).

The formulation suggests:

— Laboratory-controlled synthesis

— Access to precursor purification

— Knowledge of detonation velocity optimization

Detonation Characteristics

Estimated detonation velocity:

— Between 7,200 and 7,800 m/s (calculated)

Blast pattern analysis indicates:

— Directed energy release

— Limited secondary fragmentation

— Minimal residue dispersion beyond expected radius

This suggests:
— Precise charge placement

— Pre-calculated detonation geometry

Initiation System

No intact detonator components recovered.

However, residue patterns indicate:

— Multi-stage initiation sequence

— Use of a high-reliability primary charge

Electronic initiation cannot be confirmed or excluded.

Environmental Interaction

Soil nitration levels are consistent with:

— Short-duration high-temperature exposure

— Absence of prolonged combustion

No evidence of post-detonation fire was detected.

Comparative Analysis

The explosive composition does not match:

— Known terrorist attack profiles

— Recent cartel-related explosive incidents

— Common PMC operational munitions

No direct attribution possible based on chemical profile alone.

Conclusion:

The explosive material used in Incident 9912-47-L

was a professionally manufactured high-order explosive.

The device does not align with:

— Improvised constructions

— Opportunistic criminal activity

The origin, manufacturer, and delivery method

cannot be determined based on available evidence.

Forensic Chemistry Division

End of report.
