# Sky Lift Assessment

**“Sky Lift”**

Today marks the 6th anniversary of a “drifting lift” - a part of a [[Sky Lift]] that has been detached from its planetary foundation. Scientists are still monitoring its orbit, now confirming that it will likely stay in the solar system.

Although the object itself was deemed not dangerous to any colony or major space station, its spin is still somewhat unpredictable, which means any smaller stations or satellites on its path are likely to be in grave danger.

We have a new task for you. Your employer would like you to take a look at the Sky Lift destruction event. We don't think it was just an accident, there's something behind it. 
The only remaining traces of the incident are stored on an old [[USOL|USOL]] [[Terms#CDC|CDC]] archive node.
You'll need to connect to it through the Terminal and extract whatever files are still readable.
Your task is to uncover it and report your findings.

While you were pulling data from the USOL node, our team managed to recover one more fragment related to the Sky Lift incident.
It wasn’t stored on USOL — we found it through a separate channel, partially reconstructed from [[CORE Tech|CORE Tech]] oversight traffic.
I’ve added the file to your workspace.
Now we need to identify any individuals who might still be alive and connected to the event.
If anyone is still active in the system, we need to know.
