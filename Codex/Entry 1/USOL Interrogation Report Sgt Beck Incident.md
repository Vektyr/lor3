# THE INTERROGATION

that took place on

$$REDACTED$$

05.12.2201,

at the local [[USOL|USOL security outpost]]

---

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

I ask again, what happened to your team Sergeant ?

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]]:**

I swear to God sir, i do not know ! We were on the usual patrol, everything was as always ! Then, suddenly we all heard that shrieking sound. We all froze for a second or two. It sounded like a metal pipe was torn apart by some massive force. Then, [[Alex (interrogation)|Alex]] disappeared. He was standing behind me like 5 meters away but no one saw how it happened. He just vanished !

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

Did you search for him ? Who gave the order to separate ?

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]]:**

I think it was the Lieutenant. It was his voice, I'm sure ! But we didn't see him, only a figure walking into the bushes. It looked a bit weird from the back but no one took care of it as we were stressed anyway. So we dispersed and started searching the area.

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

How long was it until you understood that you lost all contact with the others ?

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]]:**

I am not sure, Sir. It felt like only 5 or 10 minutes passed but when I looked at my multitool the chronometer said 3 hours had passed. But I swear that the sun did not move ! The lights were all the same !

**[[Cor3a/Dossiers/Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

Focus, Sergeant ! What did you hear ? Was there any radio contact at any time ? How did you feel ? What did you do ?

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]]:**

It was all quiet, Sir. In fact, I can swear I have never been so calm in my life. I felt ... purpose. Everything around me was in slow motion. I felt the universe, I felt I knew everything in the world. It was a very pleasant but stunning feeling. So I did nothing, just stood there for a while, but as soon as it passed i'v continued my search by moving down south.

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

How did you get out of there ?

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]]:**

Well I've figured we went up north all the time so I've decided to head back. I have looked for my squad mates, but tried to keep quiet at all times. I had a feeling that someone or something was watching me. All that time that I was walking the thick fog covered the area, so I did not see anything, until I suddenly stumbled at the monorail foundation. After that I went east and was found by our patrol.

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

What else do you remember ? Do you recall the exact coordinates of the incident ?

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]]:**

No sir. I have tried to recall that but failed. It is like something is blocking me from remembering ...

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

All right Sargeant. You are dismissed.

---

**[[Beck (surname)|Sgt. Beck]] walked out of the interrogation room.**

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

Same as all of them. Memory loss and disorientation. What do the medical examinations tell us, Lieutenant ?

**[[Arthur J Kovalski|Lt. Kovalsky]]:**

As you know, we have found 3 fully decayed bodies, 2 soldiers in a state of limbo and 3 of them with light wounds and disorientation. Reports showed that they were in a squad of 10 people, initially. Our scans show residual radiation on the bodies, and some "weird" readings on all metal surfaces. I do not have an explanation. The brains of all soldiers both alive and dead have been somehow affected. But most important ... that squad has been considered MIA since 2175. My suggestion would be to proceed with further studies on all soldiers using advanced techniques of different sorts. This information might be crucial on the matter we are looking at currently ...

**[[Steven Blevsky|Major Blevsky]]:**

Heartless bastard, as always, [[Arthur J Kovalski|Mr. Kovalsky]]. Very well, proceed. And may god have mercy on our souls for that.
